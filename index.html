How to make a name tracing worksheet

Type a child's name (or any short word) in the box, choose whether you want faded gray letters to trace over or hollow outline letters to trace inside, and press Make Worksheet. Your practice sheet appears with the name repeated across dotted-midline handwriting rows. Press Print Worksheet and your browser prints a clean page — no buttons, menus, or ads on the printout.

Who uses this name tracing maker?

  • Preschool & pre-K teachers — one worksheet per child so every student practices their own name.
  • Kindergarten teachers — sight words and classmates' names for early handwriting centers.
  • Parents & homeschoolers — practice a first name, last name, or the days of the week at home.
  • Occupational therapists — large, guided letters for fine-motor and pencil-control practice.

Tips for teaching name writing

Start with UPPERCASE or Title case — a capital first letter followed by lowercase is how a name is really written, so it builds the right habit early. The dashed middle line shows where lowercase letters like a, e, and m should reach. Have the child trace slowly two or three times, then use the blank practice lines at the bottom to write the name on their own. Short names (3–6 letters) work best for beginners.

How this generator works

The name is placed onto printable handwriting guide lines — a top line, a dashed midline, and a solid baseline — then repeated to fill each row. Everything is generated right in your browser, so nothing you type is uploaded or stored. Change the name and print as many different sheets as you like, free and unlimited.
